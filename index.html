📚 Learn About Phishing & Cyber Security

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers impersonate trusted organizations to trick you into revealing sensitive information, downloading malware, or clicking malicious links. Phishing emails typically:

  • Impersonate trusted brands (banks, PayPal, Amazon, Apple, etc.)
  • Create urgency ("Verify now or your account will be locked!")
  • Ask for credentials (passwords, credit card numbers, SSN)
  • Use fake links that look real but lead to malicious sites
  • Include attachments with malware or ransomware
  • Spoof email addresses to appear legitimate

🛡️ How to Prevent Phishing

1. Check the Sender Address

Don't just look at the display name. Check the actual email address. Scammers often use addresses like paypa1.com (with a "1" instead of "l") or apple-security@gmail.com.

2. Hover Before You Click

Before clicking links in emails, hover over them to see the actual URL. If it doesn't match where it claims to go, don't click it. Malicious links often hide under official-looking text.

3. Verify Unexpected Requests

If an email claims to be from your bank, company, or service asking you to "verify your account," call them directly using a number from their official website. Don't use contact info from the email.

4. Look for Red Flags
  • Poor grammar, spelling mistakes, or odd phrasing
  • Generic greetings ("Dear Customer" instead of your name)
  • Urgent language or threats
  • Suspicious attachments or requests to enable macros
  • Mismatched logos or branding
5. Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Even if attackers steal your password, MFA prevents them from accessing your account. Enable it on email, banking, social media, and work accounts.

6. Keep Software Updated

Enable automatic updates for your operating system, browser, and apps. Security patches fix vulnerabilities that phishing links might exploit.

7. Use a Password Manager

Password managers (like Bitwarden, 1Password, LastPass) auto-fill credentials only on legitimate websites. They won't fill in your password on fake login pages.

🚨 I Think I Was Phished — What Do I Do?

Immediate Actions (First Hour)
  1. Change your password immediately from a different, clean device
  2. Enable/check MFA on the affected account
  3. Check recent activity — look for logins from unfamiliar locations
  4. Contact the organization (use official phone number, not email reply)
  5. Run antivirus scan if you downloaded an attachment
Within 24 Hours
  1. Monitor your accounts for unauthorized transactions
  2. Check credit reports at annualcreditreport.com (free in US)
  3. Place a fraud alert with credit bureaus if identity theft is suspected
  4. Report to authorities — file a report with IC3.gov (FBI) or your local police
  5. Alert your bank if financial accounts are affected
Ongoing Monitoring
  • Monitor bank statements for unauthorized charges
  • Check your credit score regularly (creditkarma.com, creditone.com)
  • Watch for suspicious emails using your compromised email address
  • Consider identity theft protection service for serious breaches

🔐 General Cyber Hygiene Practices

🔑
Strong Passwords

Use 12+ characters with uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across accounts. Let your password manager generate them.

📱
Multi-Factor Authentication

Enable 2FA/MFA on every account that offers it. Use authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy) rather than SMS when possible — SMS can be intercepted.

🖥️
Device Security

Keep OS and software updated automatically. Use antivirus (Windows Defender is fine). Enable a firewall. Use disk encryption (BitLocker, FileVault).

🔍
Browser Safety

Use updated browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge). Install ad blockers and anti-phishing extensions. Avoid public WiFi for sensitive transactions; use VPN if you must.

📧
Email Security

Don't open attachments from unknown senders. Be suspicious of unexpected emails asking for credentials. Use email filtering/rules to catch phishing.

🗑️
Secure Deletion

When disposing of devices, use secure erase (not just delete). Shred sensitive documents. Clear browser history/cache regularly.

👥
Backup Your Data

Use 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of data, 2 different media types, 1 offsite. Backups protect against ransomware and data loss.

📚
Stay Informed

Follow security news. Know about recent breaches affecting services you use. Subscribe to security alerts from your bank and email provider.

🚫
Think Before You Share

Don't share personal info (SSN, credit card, passport) via email. Don't accept friend requests from strangers. Be cautious with social media oversharing.

⚠️
Report Suspicious Activity

See phishing? Report it. Received spam? Mark it. Got a scam call? Report to FTC. Your reports help protect others.
